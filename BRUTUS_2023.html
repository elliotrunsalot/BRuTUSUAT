The Event

Inspired by the knife-like elevation profile of Mt. Coree, the stunning views over the Cotter, and an unrelated coup d'etat that happened in 44BCE, the BRuTUS 60km is an exciting new event on the ACT trail running calendar.

ULTRA Mediocre Runners of Canberra Incorporated welcomes you to enjoy this premier October ultra-distance trail race, which, though challenging, probably will not bear any particular semblance to an historical event in which a tyrant's senators, including his closest ally, stabbed him 23 times.

There are two opportunities for runners to either push beyond 50kms, or power through a sharp 15km: courses that, dramatic as they are, will not necessarily be the kind of thing Shakespeare would have written a play about.

The Races

The BRuTUS 60km/2000m elev.

The BRuTUS is a 60km course starting at the historic Blue Range Hut. It follows One Stick Road, then East West Road before crossing Brindabella Road and continuing into the Cotter Catchment Area. Runners will pass (but not cross) Vanitys Crossing before reaching CP1. After CP1 runners can expect some of the smaller hills before reaching CP2 at Curries Road where they will make the second crossing of Brindabella Road. The major climb of the course is already visible at this point and runners will take Pabral Road into NSW, then Coree Summit Road to the top. After summiting runners return down the Summit Road to Coree Campground and CP3. What follows is a long and challenging section on Two Sticks Road, followed by Blue Range Road. Runners briefly return to ACT where they will reach CP4 before proceeding back over the boarder along Ganges Trail. Off Ganges Trail lies the final obstacle, a sharp rutted Slippery Dip to Radiata Trail. Heading South on Dead Horse Gate Road, those who have run the GUMBY will find themselves in familiar territory. A gentle descent brings runners to the finish line at Blue Range Hut.

The Knife 14km/680m elev.

Taking in 14km of dirt track with 680m of accumulated elevation pain, the Knife is a mediocre serve of wild radiata pine forest and blackberry. Runners will head west on Blue Range Road before making a turn for the worst at Poo Bear Corner. The course screws then pinches on a sharp left leading to a climb known as the Lung Piercer. At the top ot the Lung Piercer runners rejoin Blue Range Road and continue to climb to the sole aid station where a marshall will grimly direct them down the Ganges Trail. Here the Knife joins the BRuTUS course for a descent more technical than most runners are prepared for, but it's the last see. At least they can be comforted by the fact that everyone who signed up for the BRuTUS will encounter this with 50kms in their legs. Eventually the trail flattens out and the route ahead looks like an easy run. But that's not where you're going. Good luck.

Race HQ and Campground

Blue Range Hut will be the Race HQ and start/finish point. The Hut is a basic facility with no power or running water.

There are composting toilets on site. Additional port-a-loos will be provided for the event.

There are no rubbish bins at the site or on course. We encourage you to take your own rubbish home with you, but we will put out rubbish bins around the hut for convenience.

There may be tank water at the hut, but it is not considered safe to drink.

Aid Stations

There will be four aid stations on course with sports drink and refreshments.

Camping

Any runners and support crew are encouraged to camp. All Camping is $10 per person per night and can booked during registration. Numbers are limited and it may book out.

Please bring food, water, toilet paper and firewood if you think you will need any of these. The nearest shops are about a 45-minute drive away at Denman Prospect

Please be advised that no pets are permitted at the campsite on race weekend.
