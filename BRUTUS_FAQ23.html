Should I sign up for the BRuTUS, or The Knife?

The BRuTUS. The Knife sounds hard. Seriously. There's a climb in it they call the "Lung Piercer".

Is there mandatory gear?

Yes. The athlete's guide will be emailed to you with specifics prior to race day.

Is there mobile or internet coverage?

Blue Range Hut sits in a valley and mobile reception and internet are patchy at best. Reception is typically better on higher points along the course.

Can I be supported by a crew?

For the BRuTUS, yes. All check points are accessible along dirt roads, and it is possible for crew to meet runners at most places on the course. Driving on the course is discouraged for the safety of runners. Please do not attempt to meet your runner after check point 4. The Ganges Trail in narrow and not maintained to the level of other fire trail in the area.

It is the organisers view that crewing for The Knife is unnecessary, however spectating is highly encouraged on the Lung Piercer and the Slippery Dip. No Vehicle access to the Slippery Dip.

I want to camp at the hut. How do I book my campsite?

Bookings can be made during the entry process.

Are cooking facilities available at the Hut?

Yes. There is a permanent gas BBQ on site.

Will there be food and water available?

We will have water, sports drink and some refreshments available at each of the checkpoints.

If I camp, can I have a campfire?

Fires are permitted only in the designated fire pits. You are not permitted to collect firewood in the nature reserve. We will have firewood available for the main fire around the hut, but if you want to use one of the other fire pits we suggest you bring some wood with you.

Where can I park?

While it is possible to park inside the locked gate, you should plan to park outside the gate along the side of the access road. People should only request to park inside the gate if the car will be parked there until the BRUTuS is over. Spaces are very limited and the gate will be locked before the start of the race.

Can I bring my caravan?

No. Blue Range Hut is set up for tent camping only. Space is limited so please don’t pitch a tent that is a house.

Can I bring my dog(s)?

Although dogs are technically permitted at Blue Range Hut, we ask that you do not bring your dog on race day.

Can I dispose of rubbish at the Hut?

Please take all your rubbish with you and pick up any litter around your campsite when you pack up. We have to ensure that the entire area is rubbish free when we leave.

Are there toilets on the course?

There are toilets at Blue Range Hut (Start/Finish) and at the Coree Campsite (CP3).

Are showers available?

No. Don't worry, you smell great.
